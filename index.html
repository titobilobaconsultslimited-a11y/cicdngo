Our Mission

Working with partners and groups within the community for the total development of individuals — especially women, youths and children — through strengthening their capacity and implementing programs, humanitarian and protection interventions, with the aim of educating, empowering, reducing poverty, and creating a stabilised, healthy and protective environment for all.

Who We Are

A Catalyst for Community Change

Center for Individual and Child Development (CICD) began operations on the 31st of May 2017. We are a non-profit organisation that serves as a catalyst for collective action — developing and delivering models that change systems and improve educational, health, social, cultural and economic conditions of individuals, especially women, youths and children in Nigerian society.

Our activities are aimed at creating a balanced society where everyone is given equal opportunity for advancement and positive development. We mobilise communities and create awareness on educational, economic, psychological, emotional and health issues for sustainable development.

From Lagos to communities across Nigeria, we have built grassroots programs that speak to the real challenges families face — violence, mental health stigma, poverty, lack of mentorship. We do not just talk about change. We walk into communities and build it together.

Our Programs

Five Programs. One Purpose.

Since 2017, CICD has developed a suite of targeted programs addressing violence, mental health, mentorship and community empowerment across Lagos and beyond.

🏅

Zero Violence Ambassadors Project

Our flagship program covers 3 Local Government Areas and 110 schools in Lagos Education District 2. Currently active in Kosofe and Somolu (with Ikorodu next), it trains young people as peer ambassadors who champion violence-free environments in their schools.

School mentors visit every Wednesday from 12–2pm using our structured curriculum, workbook and logbooks. Ambassadors spot peers in violent situations and escalate to our team for intervention. Our long-term goal: a national club of Zero Violence Ambassadors.

School Project · Community Mobilisation · Research
🎙️

Annual Workshop

Launched in 2018 to break the silence around suicide and depression in Nigeria, our Annual Workshop brings together experts, community members and affected individuals to have open, stigma-free conversations on pressing social issues.

Each edition builds on the last, evolving our approach based on field learnings and community needs. These workshops are designed to not only raise awareness — but to find lasting, actionable solutions.

Mental Health · Community · Open Dialogue
💬

Walk-In Talk Clinic

Established on 3rd August 2018, our free Talk Clinic uses talk therapy to help individuals experiencing depression or trauma identify and face their emotions — breaking the culture of silence and stigma around mental illness in Nigeria.

Originally physical, we moved the clinic online in 2020. Today, volunteer social workers, counsellors and therapists serve clients remotely, ensuring nobody faces their darkness alone — regardless of finances.

Free · Online · Volunteer-Powered
📱

Monthly Instagram Live — Mental Health

Every month, we host a live Instagram event featuring field experts and practitioners addressing mental health topics. The program helps viewers — across Nigeria and the diaspora — find clarity, healing and practical steps forward.

It is one of our most accessible interventions, reaching thousands of Nigerians who may never walk into a clinic but who are quietly struggling. Follow us @Cicdngo to join our next session.

Monthly · Instagram · Free Access
👩🏽‍🎓

Girls & Mentors — Hope Rising

Girls & Mentors (Hope Rising) provides early intervention and prevention services to 100 high-risk out-of-school young females facing poverty, domestic violence, teenage pregnancy, drug abuse, and trafficking through structured mentoring relationships.

Our 3-year strategy equips girls with career guidance, entrepreneurship skills, financial literacy, and empowers them to recognise and escape cycles of violence and exploitation — so every girl can rise.

Girls · Mentorship · Empowerment · 3-Year Plan
